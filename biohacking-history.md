## What is Technology Biohacking?

**Technology biohacking** (also called **grinding** or **wetware hacking**) refers to the practice of modifying or augmenting the human body using technology â€” typically through implantable devices, wearable sensors, or other human-machine interfaces.

### Core Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Bodily Autonomy** | The belief that individuals have the right to modify their own bodies |
| **Open Source** | Sharing knowledge, designs, and techniques freely |
| **DIY Ethos** | Empowering individuals to experiment outside institutional settings |
| **Transhumanism** | Enhancing human capabilities beyond biological limitations |

### Types of Technology Biohacking

- **Implantables** â€” RFID/NFC chips, magnets, sensors embedded under the skin
- **Wearables** â€” External devices that interface with the body
- **Sensory Augmentation** â€” Extending or adding new senses (e.g., magnetic sensing, echolocation)
- **Neural Interfaces** â€” Brain-computer interfaces and neurostimulation
- **Genetic Modification** â€” DIY gene editing (CRISPR-based experimentation)
