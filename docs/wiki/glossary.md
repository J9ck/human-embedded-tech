# Glossary

A comprehensive glossary of terms used in human embedded technology and biohacking.

---

## A

### ACR122U
A popular USB NFC reader/writer device used for reading and writing 13.56MHz NFC tags and cards.

### Aftercare
The process of caring for an implant site after installation to ensure proper healing and prevent complications.

### Antenna
The component of an RFID/NFC device that receives and transmits radio signals. In implants, this is typically a coiled wire.

---

## B

### BioGlass
Schott 8625 bioglass - the medical-grade glass used to encapsulate most RFID/NFC implants. Designed to be biocompatible and safe for long-term implantation.

### Biohacking
The practice of modifying or enhancing the human body using technology, biology, or other methods. Also known as "grinding" when referring to implants.

### Biocompatible
Materials that are compatible with living tissue, causing no harmful immune response or rejection.

### Body Modification
Any deliberate alteration to the human body for aesthetic or functional purposes, including tattoos, piercings, and implants.

---

## C

### Capacitor
An electronic component that stores electrical energy. Used in some implant circuits to stabilize power delivery.

### Captive Portal
A web page that intercepts network traffic and requires user interaction before granting internet access. Used in PirateBox/PegLeg setups.

### Chip
Common shorthand for an RFID or NFC implant.

### Clone/Cloning
The process of copying data from one RFID/NFC device to another, often used to duplicate access cards.

### Cyborg
A being with both biological and artificial (technological) parts. In biohacking, refers to humans with functional technology implants.

---

## D

### Dead Drop
A method of anonymous information exchange using a hidden physical location. Digital dead drops inspired the PegLeg project.

### DHCP
Dynamic Host Configuration Protocol - automatically assigns IP addresses to devices on a network.

### DNS
Domain Name System - translates domain names into IP addresses. In captive portals, DNS hijacking redirects all queries to a local server.

### Dual-Frequency
Implants that operate on two radio frequencies (typically 125kHz and 13.56MHz), like the NExT implant.

---

## E

### EEPROM
Electrically Erasable Programmable Read-Only Memory - non-volatile storage used in some implant chips.

### EM Field
Electromagnetic field - the physical field produced by electrically charged objects. Magnetic implants can detect AC EM fields.

### EM4100
A common 125kHz RFID protocol used in many access control systems.

### Encapsulation
The process of coating an electronic device in a protective, biocompatible material before implantation.

---

## F

### Frequency
The rate at which radio waves oscillate. RFID systems operate at specific frequencies:
- LF (Low Frequency): 125kHz
- HF (High Frequency): 13.56MHz
- UHF (Ultra High Frequency): 860-960MHz

---

## G

### Grinder
A person who practices biohacking through body modification with technological implants. The term comes from the DIY, "grind it out yourself" ethos.

### Grinding
The practice of implanting technology into the human body, typically performed outside of traditional medical settings.

---

## H

### HF (High Frequency)
Radio frequency of 13.56MHz, used by NFC and many access control systems.

### HID
A major manufacturer of access control systems. Many workplace badges use HID protocols.

### Hostapd
Host access point daemon - Linux software that turns a network interface into a WiFi access point.

---

## I

### Iceman Firmware
Community-maintained firmware for the Proxmark3, offering enhanced features and regular updates.

### Implant
Any device intentionally inserted under the skin for functional purposes.

### Implanter/Installer
A professional who performs implant procedures, typically a body modification artist or medical professional.

### Injector
A specialized needle-based device used to insert glass-encapsulated implants under the skin.

---

## L

### LED (Light Emitting Diode)
In the context of implants, an LED can be powered by RF energy to glow through the skin when scanned.

### LF (Low Frequency)
Radio frequency of 125kHz, commonly used in older RFID access control systems.

### Libnfc
An open-source library for NFC communication, used in many software projects.

---

## M

### Magnetic Implant
A neodymium magnet coated in biocompatible material and implanted under the skin, used to sense electromagnetic fields.

### Mesh Network
A decentralized network where devices connect directly to each other without centralized infrastructure.

### MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging)
A medical imaging technique using strong magnetic fields. Most implants require caution or are contraindicated for MRI scans.

---

## N

### NDEF
NFC Data Exchange Format - a standardized data format for NFC communications, allowing devices to exchange information like URLs, text, and contact info.

### Neodymium
A rare earth metal used to create powerful permanent magnets. Most magnetic implants use neodymium magnets.

### NExT
A dual-frequency implant from Dangerous Things combining NFC (xNT) and RFID (xEM) capabilities.

### NFC (Near Field Communication)
A set of communication protocols for devices within close proximity (typically 4cm or less). Operates at 13.56MHz.

### NTAG
A family of NFC chips made by NXP Semiconductors. Common variants include NTAG213, NTAG215, and NTAG216.

---

## P

### Parylene
A polymer used as a biocompatible coating for electronic devices.

### Passive Device
An electronic device that has no internal power source and is powered by external RF energy (like most RFID/NFC implants).

### PegLeg
An implantable computing project based on the Raspberry Pi Zero W, creating a subdermal wireless file sharing and communication system.

### PirateBox
An offline wireless file sharing and communication system that inspired the PegLeg project.

### PN532
A popular NFC controller chip used in many reader modules and development boards.

### Proxmark3
A versatile RFID/NFC research tool capable of reading, writing, sniffing, and emulating various card types.

---

## Q

### Qi (Wireless Charging)
An inductive power transfer standard used to wirelessly charge devices. Used to power implants like the PegLeg.

---

## R

### RC522
A common, inexpensive 13.56MHz RFID reader module used with Arduino and other microcontrollers.

### Rejection
When the body's immune system attacks an implant, potentially pushing it out of the body or causing inflammation.

### RFID (Radio Frequency Identification)
Technology using radio waves to read and capture information stored on a tag attached to an object.

---

## S

### Subdermal
Located or occurring beneath the skin.

### Sterile/Sterilization
Free from bacteria and other microorganisms. Critical for safe implant procedures.

---

## T

### T5577
A versatile RFID chip that can emulate multiple card types. Commonly used in cloning operations.

### Transdermal
Passing through the skin. Transdermal implants partially protrude through the skin surface.

### Transhumanism
A philosophical movement advocating for the use of technology to enhance human capabilities beyond typical biological limitations.

---

## U

### UID (Unique Identifier)
A unique number assigned to each RFID/NFC device, used for identification.

---

## W

### Webbing
The skin between fingers, a common location for small implants due to relatively easy access and low impact on daily activities.

### Wetware
A term for the human nervous system or biological systems interfacing with technology.

### WiFi Access Point
A device that allows wireless devices to connect to a network. The PegLeg creates one inside the body.

---

## X

### xEM
A 125kHz RFID implant from Dangerous Things, based on the T5577 chip.

### xG3
A magnetic sensing implant from Dangerous Things.

### xNT
A 13.56MHz NFC implant from Dangerous Things, based on the NTAG216 chip.

### xSIID
An NFC implant with an integrated LED from Dangerous Things.

---

## Numbers

### 125kHz
Low frequency used by older RFID access control systems.

### 13.56MHz
High frequency used by NFC and many modern access control systems.
