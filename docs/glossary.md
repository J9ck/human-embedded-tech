## A

### Access Control
Systems that regulate who can enter a space or use a resource. RFID implants can clone or replace access cards for doors, gyms, etc.

### Active Implant
An implant that has its own power source (battery or wireless charging) and can perform complex operations. Example: PegLeg.

### Aftercare
The process of caring for an implant site after installation to ensure proper healing and prevent complications.

### Antenna Coil
A wire coil inside an implant that receives electromagnetic energy from a reader and powers the chip.

### Autoclave
A device used to sterilize equipment using high-pressure steam. Essential for safe implant procedures.

---

## B

### Bioglass
Biocompatible glass used to encapsulate implants. Safe for long-term contact with body tissue.

### Biohacking
The practice of using science, technology, and self-experimentation to modify or enhance the human body.

### Biocompatible
Materials safe for long-term contact with living tissue without causing adverse reactions.

### Body Modification
Any deliberate alteration of the human body for aesthetic or functional purposes. Includes implants, piercings, tattoos, etc.

---

## C

### Capacitor
Electronic component that stores electrical energy. Used in some active implants for power stability.

### Captive Portal
A web page displayed automatically when connecting to a network. Used by PegLeg to redirect users to its interface.

### Cloning
Copying the data from one RFID/NFC device to another. Used to transfer access card data to implants.

### Coil
See [Antenna Coil](#antenna-coil).

---

## D

### Dead Drop
A method of anonymous information exchange. The PegLeg project creates a digital dead drop implanted in the body.

### DESFire
A type of MIFARE chip with enhanced security features, used in some NFC implants.

### DHCP
Dynamic Host Configuration Protocol. Used by PegLeg to assign IP addresses to connected devices.

### DIY Bio
Do-It-Yourself Biology. Citizen science and amateur biological experimentation, related to biohacking.

### DNS
Domain Name System. PegLeg uses DNS hijacking to redirect all web requests to its captive portal.

---

## E

### Electromagnetic Field (EMF)
The field produced by electrically charged objects. Magnetic implants can sense these fields.

### Encapsulation
The process of sealing an implant in biocompatible material to protect both the device and the body.

### EM4100 / EM4102
Common 125 kHz RFID chip types used in many access control systems. Can be emulated by T5577 chips.

---

## F

### Flex Implant
A flexible implant (as opposed to rigid glass) that can conform to body contours. Generally larger but with better range.

### Frequency
The rate at which radio waves oscillate. Different implant types operate at different frequencies (125 kHz, 13.56 MHz, etc.).

---

## G

### Grinder
A person who experiments with body modification technology, particularly electronic implants. Part of the biohacking community.

### Grindhouse Wetware
Organization and community focused on open-source biohacking and DIY implant development.

---

## H

### HF (High Frequency)
Radio frequency band around 13.56 MHz. Used by NFC and some RFID systems.

### HID
A major manufacturer of access control systems. HID ProxCard is a common format that can be cloned to implants.

### Hostapd
Linux software used to create WiFi access points. Used in PegLeg configuration.

---

## I

### Iceman Firmware
Popular third-party firmware for Proxmark3 devices with enhanced features for RFID/NFC work.

### Implant
Any device surgically or otherwise placed inside the body. In biohacking, typically refers to electronic devices.

### Injector
A large-gauge needle assembly used to insert some types of implants under the skin.

### ISO 14443
International standard for proximity cards (NFC). Many implants are ISO 14443 compliant.

### ISO 15693
International standard for vicinity cards. Less common in implants.

---

## J

### Journaling Filesystem
A filesystem that keeps a log (journal) of changes to prevent data corruption. Recommended for PegLeg (ext4).

---

## K

### Knife Edge
The side of the hand between thumb and index finger. A common implant placement location.

---

## L

### LED (Light Emitting Diode)
Some implants (like xSIID) include LEDs that glow when powered by an NFC reader, visible through the skin.

### LF (Low Frequency)
Radio frequency band around 125-134 kHz. Used by many access control RFID systems.

### Lighttpd
Lightweight web server software used in PegLeg and similar projects.

---

## M

### Magnetic Implant
An implant containing a permanent magnet, used for sensing electromagnetic fields.

### Mesh Network
A network where devices connect directly to each other rather than through a central point. PegLeg can participate in mesh networks.

### MIFARE
A family of NFC chips by NXP, common in transit and payment cards.

### Migration
Unwanted movement of an implant from its original position. Proper placement minimizes this risk.

### MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging)
Medical imaging using powerful magnetic fields. **Potentially dangerous for implant wearers.** See [Safety Guide](./safety-guide.md).

---

## N

### NDEF (NFC Data Exchange Format)
Standard format for storing data on NFC devices. Supports URLs, text, contacts, and more.

### Neodymium
A rare-earth element used in strong permanent magnets, including magnetic implants.

### NFC (Near Field Communication)
Short-range wireless technology operating at 13.56 MHz. Smartphone compatible.

### NTAG
A series of NFC chips by NXP. NTAG216 is common in implants (888 bytes storage).

---

## O

### OTP (One-Time Programmable)
Memory that can only be written once. Some implant areas are OTP.

---

## P

### Passive Implant
An implant with no internal power source, powered by the reader's electromagnetic field. Most common type.

### PegLeg
An implantable Raspberry Pi Zero W project that creates a wireless dead drop and mesh network.

### Piercing Needle
Large-gauge hollow needle sometimes used for implant installation.

### PirateBox
Offline file-sharing software that inspired the PegLeg project.

### PN532
Common NFC reader/writer chip used in Arduino projects.

### Position 0
Standard implant placement location in the knife edge of the hand (between thumb and index finger).

### Proxmark3
Professional-grade RFID/NFC tool used for reading, writing, and emulating cards. Essential for advanced implant work.

---

## Q

### Qi Charging
Wireless charging standard. Used to power active implants like PegLeg through the skin.

---

## R

### RC522
Budget NFC reader/writer module, common in Arduino projects.

### Read Range
The maximum distance at which an implant can be read. Typically 1-4cm for subdermal implants.

### Reader
A device that communicates with RFID/NFC implants to read or write data.

### Rejection
When the body's immune system attacks an implant as foreign material. Proper biocompatible materials minimize this risk.

### RFID (Radio Frequency Identification)
Technology using radio waves for identification. Includes both LF and HF systems.

---

## S

### Silicone
Biocompatible material used for implant encapsulation.

### Smart Card
A card with an embedded microchip. Implants can often emulate smart card functionality.

### Sterilization
Process of eliminating all microorganisms. Essential for safe implant procedures.

### Subdermal
Beneath the skin. Most biohacking implants are subdermal.

---

## T

### T5577
Versatile low-frequency RFID chip that can emulate multiple card types. Common in implants.

### TagWriter
Android app for writing NDEF data to NFC tags and implants.

### Transdermal
Through the skin. Describes implants that penetrate the skin surface (rare in biohacking).

### Transhumanist
Person or philosophy supporting the enhancement of human capabilities through technology.

---

## U

### UID (Unique Identifier)
The hardware serial number of an RFID/NFC chip. Some systems authenticate based solely on UID.

---

## V

### VCARD
Digital business card format. Can be stored on NFC implants to share contact information.

---

## W

### Webbing
The skin between fingers. Common placement location for implants.

### WiFi Access Point
A device that creates a wireless network. PegLeg functions as an implanted WiFi access point.

### Write Protection
Feature that prevents data on an implant from being modified. Can be enabled for security.

---

## X

### xEM
A 125 kHz RFID implant with T5577 chip, capable of emulating various LF card types.

### xG3
Magnetic implant from Dangerous Things, available in multiple versions.

### xNT
An NFC implant with NTAG216 chip, one of the most popular beginner implants.

### xSIID
NFC implant with NTAG I2C chip and LED indicator.

---

## Numbers & Symbols

### 125 kHz
Low-frequency band used by many access control RFID systems.

### 13.56 MHz
High-frequency band used by NFC and many smart card systems.

### 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication)
Security method requiring two forms of verification. Implants can serve as a physical authentication factor.
